Tonkatsu

Description:

Tonkatsu is a Japanese dish that consists of a breaded, deep-fried pork cutlet. It involves coating slices of pork with panko (bread crumbs), and then frying them in oil. The two main types are fillet and loin. Tonkatsu is also the basis of other dishes such as katsukarÄ“ and katsudon.

Ingredients:

  • Boneless pork loin or pork chop
  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Panko bread crumbs
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: BBQ Sauce

Cooking Steps:

  1. Dip the pork into the beaten egg and let the excess drip off.
  2. Transfer the pork to the panko bread crumbs and press firmly to create a thick layer of bread crumbs that won't flake off in the fryer.
  3. Deep fry the cutlet in 340 degrees F until fully cooked and golden brown.
  4. Rest and top with optional BBQ sauce.
